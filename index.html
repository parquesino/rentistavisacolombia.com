Frequently Asked Questions

What requirements must I meet?

You must prove a monthly passive income of at least 10 Colombian legal minimum wages. For 2026, this is COP $17,468,820 (approx. $4,742 USD or €4,012 EUR). Valid sources include rental income, dividends, annuities, or interest. Salaries and pensions do not qualify for this specific visa category.

Is insurance mandatory for this visa?

Yes. You must have a Travel All-risk Assistance Insurance. It must include accidents, illness, maternity, disability, hospitalization, death, and fundamentally, repatriation of remains. The policy must guarantee coverage in Colombia for the entire duration of your visa.

Does the visa allow me to access the public health system (EPS)?

No, the Income Holder Visa does not grant access to the public health system. You can access Colombia's excellent medical network by paying full private rates for services. Alternatively, you can contract a private local health insurance plan.

Can I work in Colombia with this visa?

No. The Income Holder (Rentista) Visa is strictly for those with passive income. You are not permitted to work for or provide services to a Colombian company. However, you can continue to manage your foreign investments.

Does this visa allow me to accumulate time for Residency?

Under the current regulations (Visitor Type V), this visa does not allow you to accumulate time towards permanent residency. It is designed for temporary stays of up to 2 years at a time.

Can I bring my family?

Yes! You can apply for beneficiary visas for your spouse and children under 25 (economically dependent). They will need their own visa applications linked to yours.
